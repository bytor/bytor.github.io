If you thought Storm in St. Petersburg was bad, you'll definitely want to pass this one up. Not only is it the exact same shows, in the exact same order, with the exact same awful sound, but the packaging is even worse. Avoid this one!

Although this does contain the entire Counterparts show, the sound quality if pitiful. The crowd comes through better than the music itself; in some places it sounds like they were yelling for the purpose of being recorded on tape. The band is very muffled and distant; the bass seems to come through pretty well, but the guitar usually doesn't.

This cd also contains several songs from an RTB show, which could have redeemed it. The sound quality of these songs is just as poor as the CP show, and sound more tinny. It's also missing most of the volume out of the left channel.

The cover is actually neat, with pictures of the band overlaid onto the inside of a car (looks to be driven on the right side, judging by the position of the steering wheel), perhaps the "red barchetta"? Other than that, there is no packaging to speak of, and since when does Alex Lifesome play in the band? And unlike most double cds, this comes in a large double case, instead of the slim flip one popular now.

This boot is the same as the "Storm in St. Petersburg" cd. Why this was released is beyond me. Don't fall into the bootlegger's trap and buy both, save your money for something worthwhile!
